This Is The Basic Violations that we should know

Basic violation:

Here are some basic violations in volleyball: Carrying or lifting the ball: If a player catches, holds, or throws the ball, it's considered a carry or lift and is a violation.

Double hit:

If a player hits the ball twice in a row, it's considered a double hit and is a violation.

Foot Foult:

If a player steps over the end line while serving or during a rally, it's considered a foot fault and is a violation.

Net violation:

If a player touches the net with any part of their body during play, it's considered a net violation and is a violation.

Back-row attack:

If a back-row player jumps and attacks the ball in front of the attack line, it's considered a back-row attack and is a violation.

Rotation violation:

If players are not in the correct serving order or rotate out of order, it's considered a rotation violation and is a violation. These are just a few examples of violations in volleyball. It's important to know the rules and regulations of the game to avoid these violations and play fair.
